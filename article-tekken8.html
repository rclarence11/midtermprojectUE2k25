They may have fallen short, but this is only the beginning. UE Zenith Esports Manila at RTX AI PC Day — January 10–11, 2026.

On January 10–11, 2026, UE Zenith Esports – Manila Campus stepped into the Tekken 8 bracket at the RTX AI PC Day powered by NVIDIA GeForce RTX. Four players. One stage. A field stacked with some of the sharpest Tekken competitors in the scene.

It wasn't the result they came for — but what happened on those two days was far from a loss. Kyro, Plazmon, Jaydee, and DiscJammer competed against top-level opponents and held their ground in one of the most intense fighting game environments Philippine collegiate esports has seen this year.

"Strength isn't everything — Jin Kazama"

// PLAZMON · KYRO · JAYDEE · DISCJAMMER — RTX AI PC Day, January 10–11, 2026

The tournament brought out elite Tekken talent, and UE Zenith's fighters didn't back down from a single match. Kyro, serving as team captain, led the squad with composure. Every set played was a lesson — a data point that this team will carry forward into every practice session that follows.

Kyro's 17th place finish as captain stands out — that placement in a loaded Tekken 8 field is a genuine benchmark. The team now has competitive data against top players, and that kind of experience cannot be replicated in practice. This is exactly how champions are built.

They will continue to show determination and dedication — to sharpen their skills, study their opponents, and come back with even more to show. The grind doesn't pause after a tournament. For UE Zenith, it only intensifies.

Thank you to Kyro, Plazmon, Jaydee, and DiscJammer for representing UE Zenith Esports on the RTX AI PC Day stage. You made the organization proud.
